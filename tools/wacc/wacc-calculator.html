Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)

What is WACC?

WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital) is a financial indicator that represents the average price a company must pay for using its capital.

WACC is used for:

  • Evaluating investment projects
  • Company valuation
  • Determining optimal capital structure

Formula for calculating WACC:

WACC = (Wd * Rd * (1 - t)) + (We * Re)

Where:

  • Wd = Weight of debt
  • Rd = Cost of debt
  • t = Tax rate on debt
  • We = Weight of equity
  • Re = Cost of equity
